Here's some information to help you understand the information we provide

UV Level Category Index Explanation
LOW "<3" You can safely enjoy being outside. Wear sunglasses on bright days. If you burn easily, cover up and use sunscreen SPF 30+. In winter, reflection off snow can nearly double UV strength.
MODERATE 3-5 Take precautions if you will be outside, such as wearing hat and sunglasses and using sunscreen SPF 30+. Reduce your exposure to the sun's most intense UV radiation by seeking shade during mid day hours.
HIGH 6-7 Protection againts sun damage is needed. Wear a wide-brimmed hat and sunglasses, use sunscreen SPF 30+, wearing long sleeved shirt and pants.
VERY HIGH 8-10 Protection againts sun damage is needed. Take steps to reduce sun exposure. A shirt, hat, and sunscreen are must, and be sure you seek shade.
EXTREME ">11" UV is dangerous and has the potential to damage skin and eyes. Avoid direct contact with UV rays and must use sunscreen, hat, long-sleeved shirt, and long pants.
